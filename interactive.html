但有一个禁忌：某些"三个一组"的数字不能涂成同一个颜色。 But there's a catch: certain groups of three numbers are forbidden from sharing the same color.

关键问题 The Key Question

数字一多，你就涂不下去了。
有一个临界点 n，到了这个 n，不管你怎么涂，都必然会触犯某条禁忌。
这个临界点就叫 Rado Number（拉多数），记作 Rk(b)。
其中 k 是你有几种颜色，b 是那个倍数参数。 As the numbers grow, you'll inevitably get stuck.
There's a tipping point n where, no matter how you color, you must violate some rule.
This tipping point is called the Rado Number, written as Rk(b).
Here k = number of colors, b = the multiplier parameter.
颜色越多（k越大），你就越容易躲避禁忌，临界点 n 就越大。
参数 b 越大，禁忌越松，临界点也越大。 More colors (larger k) = easier to dodge the rules, so the tipping point is bigger.
Larger b = looser restrictions, so the tipping point is also bigger.

背景Background 聪明的染色法：数"能除几次"The Smart Strategy: Count "How Many Times You Can Divide"

有一个非常巧妙的染色策略，能在临界点之前赢得游戏。方法就是： There's a clever coloring strategy that always wins before the tipping point. Here's how:

给每个数字一个"等级"：看它能被 b 整除几次，等级就是它的颜色。 Give each number a "level": count how many times it can be evenly divided by b. That level = its color.

什么叫"能被 b 整除几次"？就是反复除以 b，直到除不尽为止。比如： What does "how many times can you divide by b" mean? Just keep dividing by b until you can't anymore. For example:

为什么这种方法能避开所有禁忌？ Why does this strategy dodge every forbidden triple?

关键在于一个巧妙的数学矛盾。我们来一步一步看。假设有一组禁忌 (b×d, y, y+d) 全涂了同一个颜色 c： The key is a clever mathematical contradiction. Let's walk through it step by step. Suppose a forbidden triple (b×d, y, y+d) are all colored the same color c:

以 b=3 为例，假设 (3d, y, y+d) 三个数都是颜色 c：

第一步 — 看 3d：3d = 3 × d，所以 3d 比 d 多"被3整除一次"。如果 3d 的颜色（即"等级"）是 c，那么 d 的等级就是 c−1。
第二步 — 看 y：y 的颜色是 c，意味着 y 能被 3 整除 c 次。
第三步 — 看 y+d：y 能除 c 次（"高等级"），d 只能除 c−1 次（"低等级"）。把一个高等级的数和一个低等级的数加在一起，结果的等级 = 较低的那个 = c−1。

但 y+d 的颜色应该也是 c，也就是说它应该能除 c 次。矛盾！c ≠ c−1。 Take b=3. Suppose (3d, y, y+d) are all color c:

Step 1 — Look at 3d: 3d = 3 × d, so 3d is divisible by 3 one more time than d is. If 3d has level c, then d has level c−1.
Step 2 — Look at y: y has color c, meaning y is divisible by 3 exactly c times.
Step 3 — Look at y+d: y is divisible c times (high level), d only c−1 times (lower level). When you add a high-level number and a lower-level number, the result's level equals the lower one = c−1.

But y+d should also be color c, meaning divisible c times. Contradiction! c ≠ c−1.
就像混合两杯浓度不同的盐水：一杯很咸（等级 c），一杯没那么咸（等级 c−1），混合后的浓度一定达不到"很咸"的水平。所以 y+d 不可能和 y 是同等级的——它们不可能同色。 Like mixing two cups of salt water with different concentrations: one very salty (level c) and one less salty (level c−1). The mix can never reach the "very salty" level. So y+d can't be the same level as y — they can't share the same color.

这个方法对 {1, ..., bk−1} 总是有效的。所以临界点至少是 bk： This strategy always works for {1, ..., bk−1}. So the tipping point is at least bk:

Rk(b) ≥ bk
我们刚刚证明了 Rk(b) ≥ bk——临界点至少是 b 的 k 次方。但是，Rk(b) 到底等不等于 bk 呢？如果等于，就说明"能除几次"这个方法已经是最优的了。让我们从最简单的情况开始：只有 2 种颜色。 We just proved Rk(b) ≥ bk — the tipping point is at least bk. But does Rk(b) actually equal bk? If so, the "count divisions" strategy is already optimal. Let's start with the simplest case: only 2 colors.

发现 1Finding 1 多米诺效应：b 的倍数被迫同色Domino Effect: Multiples of b Are Forced Into One Color

论文的第一个新发现是：当你只有2种颜色时，b 的所有倍数会被"连锁反应"逼成同一个颜色。 The paper's first discovery: with only 2 colors, all multiples of b get forced into the same color by a chain reaction.

这就像多米诺骨牌：第一个倍数一旦选定颜色，其他倍数就全被推倒了。 It's like dominoes: once the first multiple picks a color, every other multiple gets knocked into that same color.

想象你在教室排座位，只有红蓝两种校服。一旦第一个"大个子"穿了红色，其他大个子就不得不也穿红色——因为每穿一件蓝色校服，都会立刻触发新的禁令，最终产生矛盾。 Imagine assigning uniforms with only two choices: red or blue. Once the first "big kid" wears red, every other big kid is forced to wear red too — because choosing blue immediately triggers a chain of contradictions.

一步一步看这个连锁反应 Watch the chain reaction step by step

一旦所有 b 的倍数被迫同色，你只剩一种颜色给非倍数用，但空间不够——必然出现禁忌组。
所以 2 种颜色时，临界点恰好是 b2：
R2(b) = b2 Once all multiples of b are forced into one color, only one color remains for non-multiples — but there's not enough room, so a forbidden triple is guaranteed.
Therefore, with 2 colors, the tipping point is exactly b2:
R2(b) = b2
好，2 种颜色的情况搞定了：R2(b) = b2，公式 bk 完美吻合。那3种、4种颜色呢？我们需要一个新的思路——不能再只靠 2 种颜色的多米诺效应了。 Great, the 2-color case is settled: R2(b) = b2, matching the formula bk perfectly. But what about 3 or 4 colors? We need a new approach — the 2-color domino argument no longer applies directly.

发现 2Finding 2 陷阱原理：每种颜色都有"定时炸弹"The Trap: Every Color Has a "Time Bomb"

论文的第二个发现解释了：为什么 3-4 种颜色时，临界点也恰好等于 bk？ The paper's second finding explains: why is the tipping point exactly bk for 3-4 colors too?

答案是：在临界点之前（{1, ..., bk−1}），用"能除几次"的方法染色后，每种颜色里都藏着一个"定时炸弹"——一对间距恰好为 bk−1 的同色数字。 The answer: before the tipping point ({1, ..., bk−1}), using the "count divisions" coloring, every color secretly contains a "time bomb" — a pair of same-color numbers whose gap is exactly bk−1.

想象一场选秀节目有 k 个导师，每个导师的队伍里都有两个选手站在相距恰好 100 米的位置。
现在来了一个新选手（第 bk 号），他必须选一个导师。不管他选谁，他加入后，那个导师的队伍就会有三个人形成禁忌组。
所以——新选手无处可去，游戏结束！ Imagine a talent show with k coaches, each with two members standing exactly 100 meters apart.
Now a new contestant (number bk) arrives and must pick a coach. No matter who they pick, their team will form a forbidden triple.
The new contestant has nowhere to go — game over!

有了"多米诺效应"（发现1）和"陷阱原理"（发现2），就能解释为什么 k ≤ 4 时：
Rk(b) = bk — 下界和上界恰好碰上，答案就是 b 的 k 次方。 With the "Domino Effect" (Finding 1) and the "Trap Principle" (Finding 2), we can explain why for k ≤ 4:
Rk(b) = bk — the lower bound and upper bound match perfectly, so the answer is exactly b to the k-th power.
到目前为止，所有验证过的情况都符合 Rk(b) = bk。规律这么漂亮，会不会永远成立？答案是——不是。接下来的发现令人震惊。 So far, every case we've checked satisfies Rk(b) = bk. Such a beautiful pattern — could it hold forever? The answer is — no. The next finding is a shock.

发现 3Finding 3 规律崩塌！电脑找到了漏洞The Pattern Breaks! A Computer Found the Crack

大家以前以为临界点永远等于 bk。但论文证明了一个震惊的事实： People used to think the tipping point was always bk. But the paper proved a shocking fact:

当 b=3、k=5 时：R5(3) > 243 = 35 When b=3, k=5: R5(3) > 243 = 35

也就是说，存在一种 5 色染色方案，能让 1 到 243 这 243 个数完全没有禁忌组！公式 bk 在这里失效了。 In other words, there exists a 5-color scheme that colors all 243 numbers from 1 to 243 with zero forbidden triples! The formula bk breaks down here.

这个方案是怎么找到的？靠的是一种叫 SAT 求解器的计算机程序。SAT 求解器的工作方式像这样：你给它一大堆逻辑条件（"数字 1 不能和数字 2、3 同时为红色"之类的），它会自动搜索满足所有条件的方案，或者证明不存在这样的方案。 How was this scheme found? Using a program called a SAT solver. A SAT solver works like this: you give it a huge list of logical constraints ("number 1, 2, and 3 can't all be red" etc.), and it automatically searches for an assignment satisfying all constraints, or proves no such assignment exists.

就像一个密码锁，大家以为密码公式是 bk，前面试了无数次都对。但到了第五层（k=5），密码突然不对了——锁还没开，说明真正的密码比 bk 更大。 Like a combination lock: everyone thought the formula was bk, and it worked perfectly for the first few layers. But at the fifth layer (k=5), the combination was wrong — the lock didn't open, meaning the real combination is larger than bk.

发现 4Finding 4 分界线在哪？一个精确的猜想Where's the Boundary? A Precise Conjecture

规律在 b=3, k=5 崩了。在 b=2 时，k=3 就崩了（R3(2)=14 ≠ 23=8）。论文发现了一个规律： The pattern breaks at b=3, k=5. For b=2, it breaks already at k=3 (R3(2)=14 ≠ 23=8). The paper discovered a pattern:

公式 bk 成立   当且仅当   k ≤ 2 × (b − 1) formula bk holds   if and only if   k ≤ 2 × (b − 1)
